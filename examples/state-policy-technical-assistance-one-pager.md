# Example: State Policy Technical Assistance One-Pager

Fictional and sanitized portfolio example. Not policy, legal, clinical, or funding advice.

## Topic

Improving access to youth behavioral health support through school and community referral pathways.

## Audience

State agency partners, school mental health leaders, managed care partners, community providers, and technical assistance teams.

## Shared Goal

Help young people and families move from identified need to appropriate support with fewer handoffs, less confusion, and clearer accountability.

## Current Challenge

Many systems have services available, but families and schools experience the pathway as fragmented. Common pain points include unclear eligibility, inconsistent referral expectations, limited feedback loops, and difficulty knowing when a youth needs a higher level of care.

## Technical Assistance Questions

1. What population is the pathway intended to serve?
2. What services are available now, and where are the gaps?
3. Who can identify need and initiate referral?
4. What information is required to make a referral useful?
5. What happens if the referral is incomplete or the youth is not eligible?
6. How are urgent safety concerns handled?
7. What data will show whether the pathway is working?
8. How will families know what to expect?

## Suggested Work Plan

| Phase | Focus | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Map the current state | Identify existing referral paths, handoffs, and barriers | Current-state workflow map |
| 2. Define roles | Clarify who does what across schools, plans, and providers | Role and responsibility table |
| 3. Standardize referral information | Decide what information is needed and why | Referral checklist |
| 4. Build escalation guidance | Clarify urgent, routine, and consultative pathways | Escalation guide |
| 5. Create feedback loops | Define what information returns to the referring partner | Feedback-loop protocol |
| 6. Measure and refine | Track access, timeliness, completion, and family experience | Simple dashboard or review cadence |

## Sample Measures

- Time from identified need to referral
- Time from referral to first contact
- Referral completion rate
- Percentage of referrals returned for missing information
- Reason for referral closure
- Family-reported clarity of next steps
- School or provider feedback on workflow usability

## Implementation Risks

- Referral form becomes too long to use
- Safety pathway is unclear or over-relies on one partner
- Families receive different explanations depending on entry point
- Data is collected but not reviewed on a regular cadence
- Workflow solves one team's problem by creating work for another team

## Plain-Language Success Statement

A family should be able to understand what is happening, who is responsible for the next step, and what to do if things get worse before the next appointment.

## Why this example matters

Good policy needs implementation support. I like technical assistance work because it turns a shared goal into something people can actually use: a map, a checklist, a training, a decision guide, or a review rhythm.
